# Counselor Guide

## Teaching stance

Citizenship in the Community is about moving from awareness to participation. Your job is to help scouts connect community facts, civic processes, service, and personal responsibility.

## What good work looks like

- Scout can define the community being studied.
- Research includes facts, affected people, and more than one viewpoint.
- Civic observation notes show what people discussed, debated, or decided.
- Service is connected to a real community need.
- Scout can explain how they can keep contributing.

## Red flags

- Scout copies website text without being able to explain it.
- Issue research has only one viewpoint.
- Service hours are logged but disconnected from community impact.
- Scout attended something but cannot describe what happened.

## Counselor focus

Ask scouts to connect the dots: What did you learn? Who was affected? What choices were available? What did you do? What could you do next?
