# Citizenship in the Community Traceability Matrix

This matrix explains how the kit materials map to the official badge scope without reproducing official requirement text. Use the current official requirements as the source of truth.

| Official badge area | Kit materials that support it | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Define and understand the scout's community | Workbook `Community Profile`, kickoff agenda, scout instructions | Helps scouts ground discussion in a real community instead of abstract civics. |
| Research a local issue and different viewpoints | Workbook `Issue Research`, `community-issue-research-template.csv`, touchpoint 1 agenda, question bank | Supports issue selection, affected groups, facts, sources, and tradeoffs. |
| Observe civic participation or a public meeting | Workbook `Meeting Notes`, touchpoint 1 and 2 agendas, final review agenda | Supports the meeting/civic observation requirement and reflection on public decision-making. |
| Media/movie/story showing citizenship in action | Workbook `Media Reflection`, final review checklist, final review email | Keeps the media reflection visible in final review preparation. |
| Research a community organization | Workbook `Organization Research`, touchpoint 2 agenda, final review checklist | Connects organization mission to a community need. |
| Complete meaningful service | Workbook `Service Log`, `community-service-log-template.csv`, service safety note, final review agenda | Supports service planning, adult verification, and reflection on community impact. |
| Explain good citizenship and future action | Discussion question bank, final review agenda, final review checklist | Helps the scout connect research and service to continued citizenship. |
