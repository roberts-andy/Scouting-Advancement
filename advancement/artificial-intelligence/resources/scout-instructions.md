# Scout Instructions

## Your goal

Learn what artificial intelligence can do, where it can fail, how to verify output, and how to use AI responsibly.

## What to track

- Tool, example, or AI system you explored.
- Prompt or input you used.
- Output or observation.
- What changed when you changed the prompt or input.
- What you verified and which source you used.
- Privacy, safety, bias, or ethics notes.
- Final reflection.

## Safety expectations

- Do not enter private information into public AI tools.
- Do not enter someone else's personal information.
- Follow family, school, and tool rules.
- Do not submit AI output as your own thinking.
- Always verify important claims.

## What to bring

| Meeting | Bring |
|---|---|
| Kickoff | Current official requirements and ideas for an AI example or tool to investigate. |
| Touchpoint 1 | Activity log, research notes, and early observations. |
| Touchpoint 2 | Updated log, draft demonstration or comparison, and reflection notes. |
| Final review | Completed log, research sources, demonstration/project evidence, and final reflection. |
