# Counselor Guide

## Teaching stance

Artificial Intelligence is a fast-moving topic. Your role is not to know every tool or technical detail. Your role is to help scouts ask better questions, evaluate outputs, protect privacy, and understand that AI systems require human judgment.

## What good work looks like

- The scout can explain an AI concept in their own words.
- The scout records prompts, outputs, observations, and what they changed.
- The scout verifies AI output using trustworthy sources.
- The scout identifies at least one benefit, one limitation, and one risk.
- The scout can discuss privacy, bias, accountability, and appropriate use.

## Red flags

- Scout treats AI output as automatically true.
- Scout submits AI-generated text without understanding or verifying it.
- Scout shares private information in a tool.
- Scout focuses only on novelty and avoids safety or ethics.
- Scout cannot explain what changed between attempts.

## Counselor focus

Ask scouts to compare, test, verify, and reflect. A strong final review sounds like: "Here is what I asked the AI to do, here is what it produced, here is how I checked it, here is where it was limited, and here is how a person should use judgment."

## Practical safety guidance

- Do not ask scouts to create accounts if that is not appropriate for their age, family rules, school rules, or tool terms.
- Avoid collecting screenshots that include personal information.
- Encourage scouts to use examples that are safe, general, and age-appropriate.
- Keep the conversation centered on learning and responsible use, not tool hype.
