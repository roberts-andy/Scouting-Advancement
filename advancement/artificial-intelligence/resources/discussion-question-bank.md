# Discussion Question Bank

## AI basics

- What does artificial intelligence mean in your own words?
- What is one thing AI can do well?
- What is one thing AI cannot truly understand the way a person can?
- What is the difference between a useful answer and a verified answer?

## Prompting and outputs

- How did the output change when you changed the prompt?
- What instruction made the biggest difference?
- Did the AI ever sound confident but still seem wrong?
- What information did the AI need from you to be useful?

## Accuracy and verification

- How did you check whether an AI output was accurate?
- What sources did you trust most, and why?
- What should someone do before using AI output for school, health, safety, or money decisions?
- What is one claim you would not accept without checking?

## Bias and fairness

- What does bias mean in an AI system?
- Who might be left out or misunderstood by an AI tool?
- How could training data affect results?
- What should people do if AI output seems unfair?

## Privacy and safety

- What information should you never enter into a public AI tool?
- Why does privacy matter even if you are "just testing"?
- How could someone misuse AI-generated images, audio, or text?
- What family or school rule should guide your AI use?

## Human judgment

- When should a person make the final decision instead of AI?
- What does responsible AI use look like for a scout?
- How might AI help people with disabilities, learning needs, or language barriers?
- How might AI change jobs or community life?

## Final reflection

- What surprised you most?
- What habit will you keep when using AI tools?
- What would you tell a younger scout about using AI safely?
- How did this badge change how you think about technology?
