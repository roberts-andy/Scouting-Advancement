# Kickoff Meeting Agenda

**Recommended length:** 60-75 minutes

## Goals

- Explain that this badge is about understanding, evaluating, and using AI responsibly.
- Establish safety, privacy, citation, and human-judgment expectations.
- Start the scout's AI activity log and research plan.
- Help scouts choose an AI example, tool, or scenario to investigate.

## Agenda

| Time | Topic | Counselor notes |
|---:|---|---|
| 10 min | What artificial intelligence means | Keep it practical: AI systems make predictions or generate outputs from data and patterns. |
| 15 min | Responsible use expectations | Cover privacy, personal information, school rules, accuracy checks, bias, and when not to use AI. |
| 15 min | AI examples around us | Discuss search, recommendations, maps, translation, image recognition, chat tools, accessibility, and robotics. |
| 20 min | Activity log setup | Scouts choose what they will observe, test, compare, or demonstrate. |
| 10 min | Reflection prompts | Start discussion on benefits, risks, and human oversight. |
| 5 min | Close and next steps | Confirm what to bring to Touchpoint 1. |

## Scout leaves with

- A tracking method started.
- A selected AI tool, example, or scenario to investigate.
- Clear instructions not to enter private information into AI tools.
- A plan to verify AI outputs using trustworthy sources.

## Closing script

AI can be useful, but it can also be wrong, biased, or overconfident. For this badge, your job is not just to use AI. Your job is to understand what it is doing, question the output, and explain where human judgment still matters.
