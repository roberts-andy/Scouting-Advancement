# Personal Management Traceability Matrix

This matrix explains how the kit materials map to the official badge scope without reproducing official requirement text. Use the current official requirements as the source of truth.

| Official badge area | Kit materials that support it | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Major family or personal purchase planning | `templates/major-purchase-plan-template.csv`, workbook `Major Purchase Plan`, kickoff agenda, final review agenda | Supports pricing comparison, tradeoffs, savings planning, and final discussion. |
| Budget planning and 13-week tracking | Workbook `Budget Plan`, `Transaction Log`, `Weekly Summary`; `transaction-log-template.csv`; parent kickoff email; progress nudge | Directly supports the long-running tracking requirement and discourages backfilling. |
| Budget vs. actual reflection | Workbook `Weekly Summary`, `Final Reflection`; touchpoint agendas; final review checklist | Helps scouts explain what changed, what surprised them, and what they would do differently. |
| Consumer decision-making and discussion topics | `resources/discussion-question-bank.md`, kickoff agenda, final review agenda | Supports needs/wants, buyer behavior, payment methods, and decision reflection. |
| Saving, investing, interest, risk, insurance, credit, and debt | `resources/discussion-question-bank.md`, `resources/counselor-guide.md`, final review agenda | Provides prompts for financial literacy conversations without replacing official requirements. |
| Career or future-planning discussion | Final review checklist, counselor guide, discussion prompts | Should be used with the current official requirement text and pamphlet. |
| Parent support and accountability | `emails/01-parent-and-scout-kickoff-email.md`, `emails/05-parent-progress-nudge.md` | Helps families understand that the scout owns the work while parents support reminders and realistic tracking. |
