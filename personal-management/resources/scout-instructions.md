# Scout Instructions

## Your goal

For 13 consecutive weeks, track real money choices so you can understand your habits and make better decisions.

## What to track

- Money you receive.
- Money you spend.
- Money you save.
- Money you give or set aside.
- Notes that will help you explain the choice later.

## How often to update

Update your log at least weekly. Updating more often is better. Do not wait until the end and try to remember everything.

## What to bring to meetings

| Meeting | Bring |
|---|---|
| Kickoff | Current official requirements, ideas for a major purchase, and any current budget thoughts. |
| Touchpoint 1 | Tracking log with all weeks completed so far. |
| Touchpoint 2 | Tracking log, budget comparison, and major purchase progress. |
| Final review | Complete 13-week log, budget vs. actual summary, major purchase plan, and reflection notes. |

## Final reflection prompts

- What surprised you?
- Where was your original plan wrong?
- What would you change next time?
- What money habit do you want to keep?
