# Boards of Review Parent Guide

This folder helps parents and committee members feel comfortable serving on boards of review. The goal is not to retest requirements. The goal is to have a friendly, thoughtful conversation with the scout about growth, experience, troop life, leadership, and what comes next.

Use the current official Scouts BSA requirements and your troop advancement process as the source of truth. These guides are conversation aids and intentionally do not reproduce official requirements verbatim.

## How parents should approach a board of review

- Make the scout comfortable first.
- Ask open-ended questions.
- Listen for growth, confidence, honesty, and reflection.
- Do not quiz knots, first aid steps, or memorized facts.
- Ask how the troop is working for the scout.
- Ask what adults can do to support scouts better.

## Rank folders

| Folder | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `00-scout` | Introductory rank context and optional welcome-review questions. |
| `01-tenderfoot` | Early participation, patrol identity, basic skills, and first steps toward independence. |
| `02-second-class` | Growing confidence, outdoor skills, service, and more active troop participation. |
| `03-first-class` | Becoming a self-sufficient scout ready to help newer scouts. |
| `04-star` | Transition into leadership, service, merit badges, and broader contribution. |
| `05-life` | Mature leadership, mentoring, and preparation for Eagle-level responsibility. |
| `06-eagle` | Culminating reflection on leadership, service, character, and future citizenship. |

## Simple opening script

Thanks for meeting with us. This is not a test. We want to hear about your experience, what you learned, what you enjoyed, what was hard, and how the troop can support you. There are no trick questions.

## Simple closing script

Thank you for sharing with us. We appreciate your work and your honesty. Keep thinking about how you can help your patrol, your troop, and the scouts coming behind you.
