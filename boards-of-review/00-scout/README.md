## What this rank means



Scout is the entry point into Scouts BSA. It shows that the youth is learning how the troop works, beginning to understand the Scout Oath and Law, and taking the first step from visitor to active troop member.

Many troops do not hold a formal board of review for this rank under the current program. If your troop uses a welcome conversation, this guide can help parents ask friendly questions without turning it into a test.



## Their role in the troop and patrol model



At this stage, the scout is learning where they fit. They are getting to know their patrol, learning who the youth leaders are, and beginning to understand that scouts help lead the troop while adults coach from the side.

The most important behavior is showing up, listening, trying, and asking questions.



## What they worked on



The scout has been learning the basics: troop structure, patrol identity, scout spirit, safety expectations, early outdoor readiness, and the meaning of the Scout Oath, Scout Law, motto, and slogan.



## How they should think about the next rank



Moving toward Tenderfoot means becoming more active. The scout should start practicing basic skills, participating in outings, learning how patrols work, and building the habit of bringing their handbook, being prepared, and tracking advancement.



## Questions parents can ask



- What made you want to join Scouts BSA?
- What has helped you feel welcome in the troop?
- What is your patrol name, and what do you like about your patrol?
- Who is one older scout who has helped you?
- What part of the Scout Oath or Law is easiest for you to understand?
- What part is hardest to live out?
- What are you excited to try on a campout?
- What do you need help understanding about the troop?
- What is one thing adults could do to help new scouts feel more comfortable?



## Parent reviewer reminder



Keep this conversation short and encouraging. The scout is just getting started.
