# Life Board of Review Guide

## What this rank means

Life is the final rank before Eagle. A Life scout should be a mature, active contributor who understands leadership, service, and responsibility at a deeper level.

This rank is not just a checkpoint before Eagle. It is a chance for the scout to reflect on who they are becoming and how they influence the troop.

## Their role in the troop and patrol model

A Life scout should be one of the scouts others look to for example and guidance. They may hold significant leadership roles, mentor younger scouts, teach skills, support patrol leaders, or help set the tone for the troop.

They should understand that leadership is service, not status.

## What they worked on

The scout has been building a record of leadership, service, merit badge completion, troop participation, and scout spirit. They have likely had to manage time, balance competing responsibilities, and follow through on longer-term goals.

## How they should think about the next rank

If the scout is pursuing Eagle, they should begin thinking carefully about project ideas, beneficiary needs, planning, communication, approvals, and leadership of others. They should also think about what Eagle would mean beyond the badge itself.

The next step requires initiative, organization, humility, and persistence.

## Questions parents can ask

- What does being a Life scout mean to you?
- How have younger scouts seen you lead?
- What leadership mistake taught you the most?
- What kind of service feels meaningful to you?
- How do you balance scouting with school, family, work, sports, or other commitments?
- What Eagle project ideas have you considered, if any?
- What problem in the community would you like to help solve?
- What support do you need from the troop as you move forward?
- What do you want your legacy in the troop to be?
- How has scouting shaped your character?

## Parent reviewer reminder

This board should feel more reflective and future-focused. Give the scout space to think out loud.
