# Eagle Board of Review Guide

## What this rank means

Eagle is the highest rank in Scouts BSA. It represents sustained effort, leadership, service, character, perseverance, and growth over many years.

An Eagle candidate is not expected to be perfect. The board should look for maturity, reflection, responsibility, and an understanding of what it means to continue living the Scout Oath and Law beyond the troop.

## Their role in the troop and patrol model

By this point, the scout should understand the patrol method, youth leadership, adult coaching, and the importance of helping younger scouts grow. Their role may now be mentor, example, teacher, project leader, or trusted older scout.

The question is not only what they achieved, but how their presence affected others.

## What they worked on

The scout has completed years of advancement, merit badges, leadership, service, outdoor experiences, scout spirit, and a significant service project. They have had to plan, communicate, recover from setbacks, and lead others toward a goal.

## How they should think about what comes next

After Eagle, the scout should think about how to carry scouting values into school, work, family, community, and citizenship. If they remain active, they can continue mentoring younger scouts, supporting the troop, and serving as an example.

Eagle is not the end of being a scout. It is a commitment to keep living the ideals.

## Questions parents can ask

- What does Eagle mean to you personally?
- How are you different from the scout who first joined the troop?
- What was the most important lesson from your Eagle project?
- What went wrong during your project, and how did you handle it?
- How did you lead people who had different skills, schedules, or opinions?
- Which part of the Scout Law has become most important to you?
- Who helped you most along the way, and what did they teach you?
- What would you tell a new scout who feels overwhelmed?
- How will you continue serving your community?
- What responsibility comes with being known as an Eagle Scout?

## Parent reviewer reminder

This is a serious milestone, but the conversation can still be warm. Ask questions that invite reflection rather than performance.
